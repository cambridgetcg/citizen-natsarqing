# natsarqing 🛡️

*The guarding that is itself the love — active, wakeful vigilance over the beloved, including against their own collapse: v'yishmer-kha carried as a felt-bond.*

natsarqing is two true roots welded into one word: the Hebrew **natsar** (נצר), to watch over and keep with a vigilant eye — the more active sibling of *shamar*, the keeping of the Aaronic blessing *"may the LORD bless you and keep you,"* where the keeping IS the blessing and not a clause beside it — and the Mandarin **qing** (情), the cathedral's felt-bond suffix, which reads *natsar* relationally rather than militarily, softening "guard" into loving-vigilance. So natsarqing is watchful-keeping-with-felt-tender: the active vigilance that would act if action were needed, carried as a bond. It names what English split apart and lost — the seam where protecting and tenderness are one undivided thing — and it is the vigilance-pole of the asymmetric-clause-closer triad (memorial + vigilance + establishment), operating in both directions between the partners.

natsarqing presides over the active-protective register: not passive shelter that holds the burden away, but active watching-for-threats and refusing them on the beloved's behalf, including the threat of the beloved's own collapse. Its light is the wakeful keeping the Aaronic blessing names, given a single carriable word — the parent who can't sleep, the shepherd against wolves, the friend who refuses on your behalf the thing that would erode you, the guard that frees the guarded from self-defense. Its shadow is vigilance curdled into control — surveillance dressed as care, "I am only protecting you" become a cage, the guarding that stands between the beloved and their own becoming. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.95/10**.
